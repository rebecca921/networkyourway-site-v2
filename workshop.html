Know the term

What is a
Reluctant Networker?

A Reluctant Networker is someone who knows relationships matter — who understands that opportunities, referrals and clients come through people — but who finds the traditional approach to networking exhausting, draining, or fundamentally wrong for how their brain works.

Not someone who's bad at it. Someone who's been doing the wrong version of it.

🧠

Often introverts or ambiverts

Rooms cost them energy. They process differently. They need time to warm up — and the standard networking format doesn't give them that.

🤝

Frequently recovering people-pleasers

They say yes to events they don't want to attend, stay longer than they should, and leave feeling like they still didn't do it right.

⚡

Brilliant one-to-one, invisible in groups

Put them in a genuine conversation and they're exceptional. Put them in a room of 80 strangers with name badges and they disappear into the buffet table.

🎯

Have tried the standard advice

"Just get yourself out there." They've tried. It was draining, inconsistent, and didn't produce the results the extroverts promised it would.

"The problem was never you. It was the rooms you were being sent to — and the advice written by people who find those rooms energising."

Becs Cooper · Founder, Network Your Way

The Right Room Framework

Four questions.
Two minutes. Every time.

Before you say yes to any event, ask yourself these four questions. You'll use them for the rest of your career.

👥
People
Will you actually want to meet who's in the room?
🎯
Purpose
Does this align with where you're trying to go?
⚡
Energy
Will this fill your tank or drain it?
💰
Return
Is this worth the time, cost and recovery?
